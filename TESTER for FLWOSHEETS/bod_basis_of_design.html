BOD (Basis of Design) Details

Who: Design engineers, project managers

What: Document that outlines the fundamental criteria and assumptions for the design of assets

When: During the initial design phase

Where: Design and planning departments

With: Design documents, engineering standards

How: By compiling all design criteria, assumptions, and requirements into a comprehensive document

Why: To ensure all design aspects are clearly defined and agreed upon

Quality Check:

  • Right person (engineers, managers)
  • Right time (initial design phase)
  • Right place (design departments)
  • Right equipment (documents, standards)
  • Right process (compiling criteria)
  • Right work (clear definitions)
  • Right reason (agreed design aspects)

Expanded Activities:

  • Define design criteria and assumptions for port assets.
  • Compile engineering standards and requirements.
  • Document all design parameters in a comprehensive BOD document.
  • Review and approve the BOD document with all relevant stakeholders.
  • Periodically review and update the BOD to reflect any changes in design criteria or assumptions.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Utilize guidelines from engineering design standards.
  • Case Studies: Review BOD documents from other major projects to identify effective practices.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your BOD criteria with industry standards to ensure completeness and accuracy.

Narrative: This block involves creating the Basis of Design (BOD) document that outlines all the fundamental criteria, assumptions, and requirements for the design of port assets. This ensures that all stakeholders have a clear understanding of the design parameters and that the design process is aligned with operational goals.
