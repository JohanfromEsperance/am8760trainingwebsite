Asset Hierarchy – Functional Design Specification Details

Who: Design engineers, asset managers

What: Development of a functional design specification for the asset hierarchy

When: During the design and planning phase

Where: Design and planning departments

With: Functional design specifications, asset hierarchy templates

How: By creating detailed functional design specifications that outline the hierarchical structure of the assets

Why: To provide a clear and organized structure for asset management

Quality Check:

  • Right person (engineers, managers)
  • Right time (design and planning)
  • Right place (design departments)
  • Right equipment (specifications, templates)
  • Right process (developing specifications)
  • Right work (organized structure)
  • Right reason (clear management)

Expanded Activities:

  • Define asset classes and subclasses specific to port operations (e.g., cranes, conveyor systems).
  • Develop hierarchical structure templates for organizing assets.
  • Document functional design specifications detailing the relationships and dependencies between assets.
  • Validate and review the asset hierarchy with stakeholders.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Follow the guidelines provided by the Institute of Asset Management (IAM) for developing asset hierarchies.
  • Case Studies: Examine functional design specifications from leading ports to understand how they structure their asset hierarchies.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your asset hierarchy with those from other major ports to ensure it meets industry standards and operational needs.

Narrative: This block involves developing a detailed functional design specification that outlines the hierarchical structure of port assets. This includes defining the relationships between different assets, such as container cranes, storage facilities, and support infrastructure, and creating a clear and organized structure that facilitates effective asset management and operational efficiency.
