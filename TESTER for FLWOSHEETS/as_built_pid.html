As-Built P&ID Details

Who: Design engineers, project managers

What: As-built Piping and Instrumentation Diagrams (P&ID) that reflect the actual construction of assets

When: During and after the construction phase

Where: Design and engineering departments

With: CAD software, engineering documentation

How: By updating P&ID to reflect any changes made during construction

Why: To ensure accurate documentation of the constructed assets

Quality Check:

  • Right person (engineers, managers)
  • Right time (during, after construction)
  • Right place (design, engineering departments)
  • Right equipment (CAD software, documentation)
  • Right process (updating P&ID)
  • Right work (accurate documentation)
  • Right reason (reflect actual construction)

Expanded Activities:

  • Update P&ID to reflect any changes made during the construction of port assets.
  • Verify the accuracy of as-built P&ID.
  • Document and archive the as-built P&ID for future reference.
  • Conduct periodic reviews to ensure P&ID remains accurate and up-to-date.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Follow guidelines from the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) for P&ID.
  • Case Studies: Review as-built P&ID processes from other ports to understand best practices.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your P&ID documentation with industry standards to ensure accuracy and completeness.

Narrative: This block involves creating and maintaining as-built Piping and Instrumentation Diagrams (P&ID) that accurately reflect the construction of port assets. This ensures that any changes made during construction are documented and that the P&ID accurately represents the constructed assets.
