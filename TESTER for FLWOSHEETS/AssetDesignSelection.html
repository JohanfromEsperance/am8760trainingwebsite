Asset Design and Selection

Definition: The process of designing and selecting assets based on performance, reliability, and operational requirements.

Expanded Description: Asset Design and Selection involves developing design criteria and selecting assets that meet specific performance, reliability, and operational requirements. This process includes evaluating different asset options, considering factors such as cost, lifecycle, and compatibility with existing systems. Proper asset design and selection ensure that the chosen assets align with organizational goals and contribute to efficient and reliable operations.

Key Components:

  • Design criteria and specifications
  • Evaluation of asset options
  • Cost and lifecycle analysis
  • Compatibility with existing systems
  • Documentation and approval processes
