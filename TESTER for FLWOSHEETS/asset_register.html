Asset Register Details

Who: Data analysts, asset managers

What: Comprehensive list of all assets, including details on ownership, location, condition, and remaining life

When: During asset acquisition, regularly updated

Where: Centralized asset management system

With: Asset management software, databases

How: By creating and maintaining a comprehensive asset register

Why: To ensure all assets are accounted for and organized systematically

Quality Check:

  • Right person (analysts, managers)
  • Right time (acquisition, updates)
  • Right place (management system)
  • Right equipment (software, databases)
  • Right process (creating, maintaining)
  • Right work (detailed information)
  • Right reason (systematic organization)

Expanded Activities:

  • Input and verify asset details in the register (e.g., asset ID, location, condition).
  • Maintain accuracy by regularly updating the register with new acquisitions and changes.
  • Conduct periodic audits to ensure the register is comprehensive and up-to-date.
  • Document all asset details systematically for easy reference and management.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Follow ISO 55000 guidelines for asset management.
  • Case Studies: Review asset registers from other ports to understand best practices.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your asset register with industry standards to ensure comprehensiveness and accuracy.

Narrative: This block involves creating and maintaining a comprehensive asset register that lists all port assets, including details on ownership, location, condition, and remaining life. This ensures that all assets are accounted for and organized systematically.
