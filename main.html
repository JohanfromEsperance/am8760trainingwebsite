Work Management System Details

Who: Operations managers, maintenance planners

What: System for managing work orders and maintenance tasks

When: Continuously, with regular updates

Where: Centralized management system

With: CMMS, work management software

How: By organizing, scheduling, and tracking work orders and maintenance tasks

Why: To ensure efficient execution of maintenance tasks and proper resource allocation

Quality Check:

  • Right person (managers, planners)
  • Right time (continuously)
  • Right place (management system)
  • Right equipment (CMMS, software)
  • Right process (organizing, scheduling)
  • Right work (managing tasks)
  • Right reason (efficiency and resource allocation)

Expanded Activities:

  • Organize and prioritize work orders based on operational needs.
  • Schedule maintenance tasks and allocate resources using CMMS.
  • Track progress and completion of work orders.
  • Review and update work management processes based on performance data.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Follow guidelines from the Institute of Asset Management (IAM) for work management.
  • Case Studies: Review work management practices from other ports to identify effective strategies.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your work management practices with industry standards to ensure efficiency.

Narrative: This block involves implementing a work management system to organize, schedule, and track work orders and maintenance tasks for port assets. By using CMMS and other work management software, ports can ensure that maintenance tasks are performed efficiently and that resources are allocated effectively.

RACI Details

Who: Project managers, team leads

What: Framework for defining roles and responsibilities in asset management

When: During project planning and periodically reviewed

Where: Project management and operations departments

With: RACI matrices, project management software

How: By developing and implementing a RACI framework for asset management activities

Why: To ensure clarity in roles and responsibilities and improve coordination

Quality Check:

  • Right person (managers, leads)
  • Right time (planning, reviews)
  • Right place (management, operations)
  • Right equipment (matrices, software)
  • Right process (defining roles)
  • Right work (clear responsibilities)
  • Right reason (improve coordination)

Expanded Activities:

  • Develop a RACI matrix to define roles and responsibilities for asset management activities.
  • Implement the RACI framework in project management software.
  • Communicate roles and responsibilities to all team members.
  • Review and update the RACI matrix based on project changes and feedback.

Research and Benchmarking:

  • Best Practices: Follow guidelines from the Project Management Institute (PMI) for developing RACI frameworks.
  • Case Studies: Review RACI implementations from other ports to identify effective practices.
  • Benchmark Data: Compare your RACI framework with industry standards to ensure clarity and coordination.

Narrative: This block involves developing and implementing a RACI framework for asset management activities. This ensures clarity in roles and responsibilities and improves coordination among team members.
