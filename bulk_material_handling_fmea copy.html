NOTES:
 
Failure Mode: The specific way in which an asset might fail.
Potential Effects: The consequences of the failure mode.
Severity (1-10): The seriousness of the effect of the failure.
Potential Causes: Reasons why the failure might occur.
Occurrence (1-10): The likelihood that the failure will happen.
Current Controls: Existing measures in place to detect or prevent the failure.
Detection (1-10): The ability to detect the failure before it occurs.
Risk Priority Number (RPN): A numeric assessment of risk, calculated as Severity x Occurrence x Detection.
Recommended Actions: Suggested measures to reduce the risk of failure.
 
This FMEA table for bulk material handling systems identifies potential failure modes, their effects, causes, and the current controls in place. It also provides recommendations for mitigating risks associated with these failures. The Risk Priority Number (RPN) helps prioritize which failure modes should be addressed first
